ABSENCE MANAGEMENT
Policy Statement
XYZ expects regular and reliable attendance as a fundamental employment obligation. At the same time, the organisation recognises that legitimate circumstances may prevent attendance and therefore provides structured mechanisms to manage both short-term and extended absence.
Absence management aims to balance employee wellbeing, operational continuity, workforce planning stability, and fairness in application. Patterns of absence may indicate broader issues including workload imbalance, health concerns, workplace conflict, or disengagement, and will be assessed accordingly.
This policy applies to all categories of employees regardless of grade, tenure, or working model.
Operational Coverage
This section governs:
Short-term illness
Long-term medical absence
Intermittent absence patterns
Emergency personal leave
Unauthorised absence
Return-to-work review
Attendance monitoring thresholds
Rules
Employees must notify their direct manager at least 30 minutes before their scheduled start time unless physically incapacitated.
Absence exceeding 2 consecutive working days requires submission of a self-declaration form within 24 hours of return.
Any absence extending beyond 5 consecutive working days must be supported by medical certification issued no later than the 7th calendar day of absence.
Three separate absence instances within a rolling 90-day period may trigger a formal attendance review meeting.
Where total absence exceeds 8 working days within a rolling 6-month window, management may initiate an attendance improvement plan.
Absence of 10 consecutive working days without contact may be treated as voluntary abandonment of employment, subject to documented outreach attempts on at least two separate days.
Long-term absence exceeding 30 calendar days will require a structured case review every 30 days thereafter.
Employees on medical absence exceeding 45 calendar days may be required to participate in a fitness-for-work assessment prior to resuming duties.
Return-to-work discussions must occur within 48 hours of resumption following any absence exceeding 3 working days.
Employees are expected to maintain reasonable contact during extended absence, typically not less than once every 14 calendar days unless otherwise agreed.
Adjusted or phased return arrangements may be granted for a period not normally exceeding 20 working days, unless extended by management discretion.
Repeated absence adjacent to public holidays or scheduled leave occurring more than twice within a 12-month period may be subject to review.
Failure to provide accurate medical documentation within required timelines may delay pay processing.
Absence data will be reviewed quarterly at departmental level, and any unit exceeding a 6% absence rate may require corrective action planning.
Deliberate misrepresentation of illness or incapacity constitutes misconduct and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
ANNUAL LEAVE
Policy Statement
XYZ provides annual leave to ensure sustained employee wellbeing, prevent burnout, and promote long-term productivity. Leave must be scheduled in a manner that supports both individual rest and operational continuity.
Annual leave is an entitlement subject to planning discipline, business coverage requirements, and documented approval processes. Employees share responsibility with their managers for ensuring leave is utilised responsibly and within defined cycles.
Operational Coverage
Leave accrual
Scheduling and approval
Carry-forward limits
Peak-period planning
Leave during notice
Cancellation and modification
Rules
Employees must submit leave requests at least 10 working days in advance for leave of up to 5 working days.
Any single leave request exceeding 5 consecutive working days requires submission at least 20 working days in advance and second-level approval.
Leave requests exceeding 10 consecutive working days require departmental head approval and confirmation of workload coverage planning.
No more than 25% of employees within the same functional unit may be on leave simultaneously unless explicitly authorised.
Employees must utilise at least 50% of their annual entitlement within the first three quarters of the leave cycle.
A minimum continuous leave block of 5 working days must be taken each leave cycle unless operationally impractical.
Carry-forward of unused leave is limited to 5 working days and must be utilised within 90 calendar days of the new cycle.
Accumulated unused leave exceeding 8 days at year-end may be subject to forfeiture unless previously approved for deferment.
Leave may not ordinarily be taken during the final 30 calendar days of employment unless approved by HR.
Approved leave may be withdrawn by management in circumstances of material business disruption, with a minimum of 3 working days' notice where feasible.
Employees on unpaid leave exceeding 30 consecutive days will not accrue annual leave during that period.
Leave requests submitted during officially declared peak operational periods may be deferred without guarantee of alternative dates.
Employees are expected to avoid engaging in paid external employment during annual leave where it may reasonably impact fitness for duty upon return.
More than two cancellations of approved leave within a 12-month period may require managerial review.
Failure to return from leave on the agreed date without notification exceeding 1 working day may be treated as unauthorised absence.

This policy applies to all AI-enabled tools used for recruitment, performance analysis, workforce planning, productivity monitoring, and operational automation.

Algorithmic bias mitigation

Data governance controls
Monitoring and transparency
Acceptable use standards
Rules

AI tools used in recruitment screening must not independently reject candidates without human review for at least the first 12 months of implementation.
AI-generated performance scores must be reviewed by a line manager before being formally recorded.
Automated productivity monitoring exceeding 20% of an employee’s measurable output metrics must be disclosed in writing.
AI tools must not process personal data beyond the originally declared purpose without secondary approval.
Any AI system demonstrating error rates exceeding 5% in audit sampling must be suspended pending review.
Employees must be informed within 30 calendar days of introduction of any AI system materially affecting their role.
Algorithmic adjustments affecting more than 10% of workforce scheduling patterns require senior leadership review.

Data used to train internal AI models must be reviewed for bias at least annually.
AI-generated disciplinary flags must not automatically trigger formal proceedings without managerial verification.
External AI vendors must provide documented model transparency summaries prior to engagement.
Employees may request clarification on AI-generated outputs impacting their employment status.
AI tools must not be used for covert monitoring without executive approval.
Significant AI failures resulting in operational disruption exceeding 8 working hours must be formally reported.
